# Small Business Operator Migration

## Scenario

A small business operator is moving to a new Mac used for daily operations. Important items include client or vendor documents, email, calendar, contacts, browser profiles, password manager access, finance or scheduling apps, messaging apps, printer or scanner setup, and recurring automation checks.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Complete the intake form with operational workflows instead of only folder names.
2. Freeze the source Mac after confirming recent business files, email, calendar, and app data are current.
3. Choose the transfer method that best preserves user account data, apps, documents, cloud sync state, and any external media.
4. Validate first boot basics, then validate one real work task per critical workflow.
5. Record proof in the ledger with clear labels and absolute dates.
6. For any gap, identify whether the source Mac, cloud service, backup, or app account is the system of record before repairing.
7. Keep repair steps narrow and documented. Do not delete conflict copies or overwrite business folders during validation.

## Validation Checklist

- Source Mac is still available for rollback during at least one normal business cycle.
- Email, calendar, contacts, browser profiles, and password manager access are checked without exposing private data.
- Critical folders open and recent business files are present.
- Finance, scheduling, messaging, document, printer, and scanner workflows are checked if they matter to the operator.
- Cloud services show the expected account labels and sync status.
- Any app requiring sign-in is marked `blocked` until the operator confirms access.
- External drives or shared folders used by the business are mounted and checked separately.
- Proof ledger notes separate confirmed facts, user-confirmed facts, inferences, missing evidence, and blockers.

## Retirement Gate

Do not retire the source Mac until the operator has completed the agreed business workflow checks on the new Mac and the proof ledger has no unresolved critical `missing` or `blocked` items. The retirement packet should state what was proven, what can wait, what risk remains, and what action the operator is approving for the source Mac.
